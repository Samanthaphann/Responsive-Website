Alcatraz Island

When did it open?

Alcatraz opened on August 11, 1934. The Federal Government decided to open it to show the public that they were serious about the rampant crime happening in the 20s and 30s. They picked the location because the rough currents and high temperatures would make it difficult to escape.

When did it close?

Alcatraz closed on March 21, 1963 after operating for 29 years. It closed due to it being too expensive to operate. It costed $10 per prisoner per day in Alcatraz. That's 3 times more as much as running other federal prisons at that time.

Escapes

John Angin, Frank Morris, and Clarence Morris are the only people that sucesssfully escaped Alcatraz. They escaped by making paper mache replicas of their heads. What happened to still remains unknown and a mystery.

Now

Alcatraz is now used as a tourist site and musuem. Approximately 4,500 tourists visit Alcatraz daily. The costs of operating the island is still a problem till this day
